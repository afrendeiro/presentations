The Problem

Developing drugs successfully is costly and hard.

Many drugs fail at various points during development.

It is hard to predict the efficacy of a drug in humans.

The Opportunity

The human genome, the advent of biotechnology and GWAS:

  • identify genes influencing human health and disease
  • identify best targets and indications for each drug

Discussion

Under/overestimation?

  • all gene associations were used- but are they all causal?
  • on the other hand, aren't we still missing many more associations?

There might be drugs targeting the right gene, but we don't know their target still.

Contribution of genetic support may be underestimated simply due to the time drugs take to develop.

Data missing from failed drugs - why did they fail?

A call for (increased) drug repurposing?
